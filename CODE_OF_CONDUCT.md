# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in Astro Admin Dashboard a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of background, identity, appearance, experience level, or ability.

## Expected behavior

Examples of positive behavior include:

- Communicating with empathy, patience, and respect
- Giving and accepting constructive technical feedback
- Focusing discussions on what is best for the project and its community
- Respecting different viewpoints and levels of experience
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them

## Unacceptable behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable:

- Harassment, intimidation, discrimination, or personal attacks
- Trolling, insulting comments, or sustained disruption
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted attention
- Publishing another person's private information without permission
- Threats of violence or encouragement of harmful behavior
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional community

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, and other public or private communication related to the project. It also applies when someone officially represents the project elsewhere.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior privately to [andrixngoyi243@gmail.com](mailto:andrixngoyi243@gmail.com). Do not open a public issue for sensitive reports.

Reports will be reviewed promptly and handled as confidentially as reasonably possible. Project maintainers may remove or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

## Enforcement guidelines

Depending on the impact and context, maintainers may take one or more of the following actions:

1. **Correction** — A private or public request to change inappropriate behavior.
2. **Warning** — A formal warning with clear consequences for continued behavior.
3. **Temporary restriction** — A temporary ban from project interaction.
4. **Permanent restriction** — Permanent removal from project spaces for serious or repeated violations.

Maintainers responsible for enforcement must avoid conflicts of interest and apply these guidelines fairly.
